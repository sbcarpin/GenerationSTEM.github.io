Our Mission

To help future generations and the underrepresented. To bring more diversity in majors and work fields in STEM and Computer Science.

Our Vision

Hope that underrespresented communities have equal oppurtunity to learn about STEM especially about Computer Science
What does STEM education for everyone mean to us?

We believe that everyone should have an equal opportunity with STEM education. From research we have observed how undeerrepresented and more diverse communities have a lack of STEM education compared to other communites. We want to take a step to change that by offering this education to those communities. By starting at an early age, we can encourage students to purse STEM careers to bring equality and diversity in the work field.

Roots of the Program

The program began as a senior project at Cal Poly SLO by a student, Emily Anderson, who had a passion to help underrepresented communities. She founded the program with a different handbook based off from her experience running the program. After she graduated, three other Cal Poly students (Louisa, Stephanie, and Aurabel) took the project as a Senior Project. We researched more and decided to change the program to how it currently is.

We noticed the need for more STEM education programs in the community of Santa Maria. We have seen through our few program runs the difference it makes to young individuals. Everytime we run the program we see how we can improve it to better help the community.

We all have a passion to help the future generations especially in STEM education. We hope that our program can create a long term difference in students and the community. We hope it unites the communities as well.
