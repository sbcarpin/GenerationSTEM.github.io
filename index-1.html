Welcome Teachers!

Program Overview

Generation STEM seeks to create an interactive, academic experience for students to learn and explore problem solving skills and techniques utilized in the computer science discipline. Students will learn about teamwork as they work in a group to accomplish a goal. Students will help a Cue robot complete an adventure while learing about computer science and putting STEAM skills into practice.

Lesson Plan

Classes are introduced to the program by explaining it or playing the 2 minute introduction animation in the students page. Then classes work in pairs to do the engineering design activies. Finally, classes are split into groups of four to five students, with each group using a device equipped with the Cue robot application and a Wonder Workshop Cue Robot. Each robot is assigned a persona, obstacle course, and mission that can be found here. The ultimate goal of the activity is for groups to code their robot’s path through their obstacle course, effectively achieving their narrative’s mission. Implementation

The two engineering design activities were designed to exercise key skills needed to be successful in the field of engineering; such as teamwork, logical problem-solving, and creativity. The activities chosen require various technical and creative skills in the hopes that all students will participate in an activity which intrigues them, plays on their existing skills, and teaches them new ones.

Groups will receive activity instruction cards which detail their robot’s mission. Groups are given a coding instruction card which guides them in writing code to animate their robot. Groups will return to their respective robot and obstacle course to input code on the Cue app. Groups will recieve narractive cards for the beginning and ending of the robots. The materials for each robot is found in the materials page. Encouragement of the engineering design process is told throughout the whole session.

Engineering Design Process

Engineering Design Activites

The two engineering design activities were designed to exercise key skills needed to be successful in the field of engineering; such as teamwork, logical problem-solving, and creativity. The activities chosen require various technical and creative skills in the hopes that all students will participate in an activity which intrigues them, plays on their existing skills, and teaches them new ones.

Activity 1: Index Card Tower

Students should be put into pairs with the person sitting next to them. Each pair is given a sheet of engineering paper, 30 standard index cards, 30 inches of tape, and 10 minutes to build the tallest index tower possible. Pairs are required to spend the first two minutes working together to plan out their design idea on the engineering paper before they were allowed to begin building. Students are updated on remaining time with 5 minutes, 3 minutes, and 1 minute left. At the end of the 10 minutes, the towers are measured with a yard sticks to determine the winner. The winners are the pair that build the tallest tower.

Goal: Build the tallest tower possible given the constraints

Constraints: 30 index cards; 30 inches of tape; 10 minutes

Activity 2: Popsicle Stick Bridge

Start by moving each student pair’s desks 12 inches apart. Students work with the same partner they worked with for the Index Card Tower challenge. Each pair is given a sheet of engineering paper, 30 jumbo popsicle sticks, 30 inches of tape, and 10 minutes to build the strongest bridge possible. Pairs are required to spend the first two minutes working together to plan out their design idea on the engineering paper before they were allowed to begin building. Students are updated on remaining time with 5 minutes, 3 minutes, and 1 minute left. At the end of the 10 minutes, the towers are tested for strength with 1lb sandbags. The winners are the pair whose bridge cansupport the most sandbags.

Goal: Build a bridge that can hold the most weight given the constraints

Constraints: 30 popsicle sticks; 30 inches of tape; 10 minutes

Recommendations

  • Allow for one hour of preparation time
    • To ensure that all materials are properly configured, it is important to accommodate for one hour of preparatory work prior to student participation.
  • Assemble a team of at least seven members
    • Student participation is maximized if every student group is assisted by a supervising facilitator. It is recommended that at least seven team members are present for any implementation of this program.
  • At least one Spanish speaking team member
    • To ensure all students are actively engaged in the material, it is highly recommended that one supervising facilitator on the team be a fluent Spanish speaker. Many students participating in the program learned Spanish as their first language and are currently learning English. Providing students with a resource such as this will significantly improve the effectiveness of the program.
  • Plan for a two hour lesson
  • Have an agenda
    • Introduction: Provide an in-depth description of the program’s goals and flow
    • Engineering Design Activities: Run the acitivty but keep track of time
    • Tutorial: Provide a visual tutorial of the Cue robot application. Use a coding instruction card to walk students through implementing code.
    • Split into groups: Student groups should have 4 members; 5 maximum. Assign supervising facilitator to each group
    • Program participation: Use a timer to facilitate program flow. An hour for engineering design activites. An hour for coding portion.
